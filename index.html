I'm a PhD candidate at the University of Potsdam and the Macroeconomic Policy Institute (IMK).

My research focuses on the intersection of macroeconomics and environmental policies. In my disseration, I look at the effects of fiscal policies that are aligned with the transition of the economy towards climate neutrality. In my first chapter, I analyze the effects of carbon prices on inflation and how sectoral price shocks can affect the overal price level via input-output linkages. In my Job Market Paper, I empirically estimate the employment effects of green government spending through the production network.

Prior to my PhD, I worked as a researcher at Ecologic Institute, where I conducted research on green industrial policies, emissions trading systems and other economic topics related to sustainability.
